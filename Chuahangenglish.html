KAMPONYNIXRDLE PAGODA

The pagoda was established in 1637 and has experienced 22 generations of abbots.

Address

Located in Hamlet 3, Chau Thanh town, more than 5 km south of Tra Vinh city center, along Highway 54.

History of Construction

he pagoda was established in 1637 and has experienced 22 generations of abbots. In 1968 during the Mau Than Tet, the pagoda was heavily damaged by bombs. In 1977, monk Thach Suong (now the 23rd abbot) returned, and campaigned to rebuild the pagoda. Since then, through many restorations, the pagoda has become splendid and spacious.

Architecture

Originally, the pagoda faced the east, where there was a boat dock near the banyan tree for local people to conveniently go there to listen to scriptures and worship Buddha, so it was named Wat Kompong Ch'ray (Banyan Pagoda). Towards the beginning of the twentieth century, an extra gate of the pagoda was built to the west, facing Provincial Road 36 (now National Highway 54). This side gate is in the form of a dome with two small caves on both sides and a larger cave in the middle, so the pagoda has the name Hang Pagoda/Cave Pagoda.
The gate: is a "trait" showing that Tra Vinh Khmer Theravada Buddhism has a certain inheritance of Brahminism, because in the past, Brahmin monks used to quietly lead the religious life in caves.
The main: hall of the pagoda is built on a high ground, with many steps leading up, splendidly decorated with many patterns and motifs.
The roof of the main: hall is composed of many overlapping layers, on the top being a super high majestic tower peak.
Inside the main: hall are two rows of tall and majestic columns; in the middle of the main hall is the altar. The large statue of Shakyamuni Buddha is placed on a high platform with smaller Shakyamuni Buddha statues in many different postures placed below.
In front of the guest house and the monastery is a flagpole tower, the foot of the tower has a relief set of 7 Naga snake heads. Opposite the main hall of Hang Pagoda is a population of 12 animals of zodiac, arranged in an arc shape embossed pairs of dragons winding like a boat.
The campus of Hang The campus of Hang Pagoda is about 7 hectares wide, a really primeval forest with many endemic plant species such as golden oaks, balsam trees, bamboos, small bamboos ... crammed into many different high and low levels. Today, on the pagoda campus are nearly tens of thousands of birds of all kinds, most of which are white storks, ivory storks, red-necked storks and herons. Loved and protected by humans, birds are getting more and more fearless.
More information about Hang Pagoda: In the pagoda, there is a fairly large school and about twenty small stilt houses, each of which housing just one person. Khmer people in the area often come to pray and gather. In addition, in Hang Pagoda, there is also an artistic wood carving workshop that has been operating for nearly 30 years and has produced many works of different sizes.
