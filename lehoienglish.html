Among the festivals and cultural events held annually in Tra Vinh, the three festivals that attract the most visitors are: Ok Om Bok festival of Khmer people, Vu Lan Thang Hoi or also known as Ong Bon worshiping ceremony. of the Chinese, and the Nghinh Ong ceremony of the Kinh people. The annual Ok Om Bok festival is held at Ba Om Pond, belonging to the complex of relics of Ao Ba Om - Ang Pagoda - Museum of Khmer Ethnic Culture.

Ok Om Bok Festival

Ok Om Bok Festival, also known as Moon Worshiping Festival, is one of the three main festivals of the Khmer people in the South (Sene Dolta, Ok Om Bok and Chol Chnam Thmay), held in the occasion of full moon in the 10th month of the lunar calendar every year. It is the time when the rainy season turns into the dry season, or the planting season turns into the harvest season. According to Khmer belief, the moon is the god who governs the weather and the crops of the year. The ceremony of worshiping the Moon is usually held in the village, in the yard of the pagoda, or in the yard of the house. The Khmer people thank the Moon god for giving them favorable rain and wind in the year, and for helping the harvest to be bountiful and they pray for the weather to be favorable, in order to have a good harvest the next year.

Every year, on the occasion of Ok Om Bok festival, thousands of Khmer people gather at Ba Om Pond cultural relic and this festival attracts tourists from all over the place to participate in the festival. Here, in addition to the ceremony of worshiping the Moon, other exciting activities are also conducted by the people, the most prominent of which is Ngo boat racing and water lantern releasing. The Ngo boat, about 20m long and 1.2m wide, can carry 40-60 people; each boat is decorated with colorful patterns, and has an animal symbol at the top of the boat. Ngo boat racing is considered a typical cultural and sports event of the Khmer people. The ceremony of releasing water lanterns - a type of lantern made of banana stems and sheaths, with the offerings inside, is meant to commemorate the footprints of the Buddha on the "Na Mi Thi" river.

In 2014, the Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism recognized the Ok Om Bok festival of the Khmer people in Tra Vinh province as a national intangible cultural heritage.
