THE PEDESTAL OF THE STATUE OF GAUTAMA BUDDHA

Material: Wood

Dimensions: 36cm in height, 44cm in width

Dated: Late 19th century

Place of discovery: Bodhinaga Pagoda, Kim Cau hamlet, Kim Hoa commune, Cau Ngang district, Tra Vinh province

Voice-over:

View 3D artifacts
×

Artifact information:

The pedestal of the statue is designed with four floors, which is the place where Gautama Buddha sits in a meditative position. The pedestal of the statue is carved with decorative motifs such as scrolled leaves, lotus petals, and grass patterns, combined with four-petal flowers painting. In front of the pedestal of the statue is a long two-layered brassiere with a wood-carving covering the bottom strip, with the upper layer of the pedestal of the statue covering down a triangle, touching 1/2 of a 4-petal flower. The long layer on the lower part of the brassiere is carved with scrolled leaves.
