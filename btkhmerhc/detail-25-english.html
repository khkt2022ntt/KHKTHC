THE LINGA - YONI IDOL SET

Material: Glass stone

Dimensions: Height: 2.1 cm; Diameter: 3.7 cm, Weight: 150 grams

Date: 5th - 6th centuries AD

Place of discovery: The Linga - Yoni idol set of Tra Vinh General Museum excavated by archaeologists in December 1986 at the archaeological site of Luu Cu II, Luu Karma Anh commune, Tra Cu district, Tra Vinh province.

Artifact information:

Recognition: As a National Treasure according to Decision No. 2496/QD - TTg dated December 22, 2016 of the Prime Minister.

The Linga - Yoni idol set is handcrafted with quite sophisticated glass stone material, using a hewn technique and then polished, creating a complete worshiping mascot symbol. This is a unique and rare artifact of special value. In terms of history, contributing to demonstrate the Oc Eo culture in Tra Vinh as well as in the South; In terms of culture, the relic of Luu Cu II is proven to be the Shiva Temple in the development stage of the highly artistic Oc culture; About science is a document for many scientists of many different fields to study under many separate or interdisciplinary perspectives... Artifacts have contributed to affirming an Oc Eo culture that once existed and flourished in the Southern region in general and Tra Vinh in particular, and also contributed to determining the existence of the kingdom of Funan. in the South is famous in the world.
