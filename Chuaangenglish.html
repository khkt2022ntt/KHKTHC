ANG KOR RAIG BOREI PAGODA

Ang pagoda was built at the end of the 10th century (1534 Buddhist calendar, i.e. 990 solar calendar)

On 12/7/1994, the pagoda was ranked by the Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism as a national relic of architectural and artistic type.

Address:

More than 5 km from the center southwest of Tra Vinh city, in Hamlet 4, Ward 8, Tra Vinh city, Tra Vinh province.

History of Construction:

According to legend, Ang pagoda was built at the end of the 10th century (1534 Buddhist calendar, i.e. 990 solar calendar). However, the main hall with unique architectural values ​​still exists today, built in 2386 Buddhist calendar, i.e. 1842 solar calendar.

Architecture:

The gate of the pagoda: Located in the east and decorated in the form of yăk (ogres), krud (divine birds), key-no (fairies), ....
Main hall: with an area of ​​864 m2, located in the center of the pagoda on a high platform and a wide corridor on 4 sides, with entrance gates in both east and west directions.
Main hall frame: made of precious wood
Roof: including 3 levels, the top 2 roofs are steeper and higher than the other one. On the roof fringes, there is a phu-chong (dragon) shape stretching along the strip.
Interior: a place to worship Buddhas with 12 main wooden columns on which there are phu-chong shapes. The main Buddha statue is 2.1m high, sitting on a lotus throne, surrounded by 50 large and small Buddha statues. The front is a very sharp carved wooden fresco style painted door. The four walls are decorated with many paintings about the life of Buddha.
In front of the main hall: is a tower with 5 tops, at the base of the tower are 4 Rech-cha-sey (King lions).
Around the main hall: are many towers, on the top of the tower is the head of the 4-faced god Ma-ha-prum. In addition, on the pagoda grounds, there are many other architectural works such as sala, monks, towers for bone ashes, etc.
